Flagship Work

Tumor Vascular Atlas

Single-cell dissection of tumor vascular heterogeneity and angiogenic programs

Our comprehensive single-cell atlas work reveals unprecedented diversity in tumor vasculature, mapping distinct endothelial subpopulations with unique molecular signatures. This research establishes my capability to construct high-resolution cellular maps and derive biological insights from complex datasets.

Key Contributions:

  • • First single-cell atlas of tumor vasculature across multiple cancer types
  • • Identification of vessel normalization programs predictive of immunogenicity
  • • Development of computational frameworks for vascular heterogeneity analysis

PDAC Ecosystem Heterogeneity

Large-scale single-cell and spatial dissection of pancreatic cancer

By integrating single-cell RNA-seq with spatial transcriptomics, we map the remarkable diversity of the PDAC microenvironment. This systems biology approach identifies distinct ecosystem ecotypes with unique cellular compositions and spatial architectures that drive clinical outcomes.

PANCOSMOS: An open-access interactive resource platform enabling exploration of PDAC ecosystem heterogeneity and its clinical implications.

Impact: 100+ researchers using PANCOSMOS for PDAC research (2024-2026)

Mechanism and Translational Insights

From spatial biology to therapeutic hypotheses

Moving beyond descriptive atlases, our mechanistic studies uncover the biological principles governing cellular interactions within the tumor microenvironment. This translational research bridges discovery science with clinical applications.

Current Focus: The ADA2-TLS immunometabolic axis where spatial transcriptomics reveals metabolic crosstalk between tumor cells and immune cells, identifying novel targets for combination therapies.
